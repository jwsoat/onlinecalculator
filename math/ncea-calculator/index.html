How NCEA credits work

NCEA is a credit-based qualification. Every internal or external assessment ("standard") is worth a fixed number of credits, and each credit is graded Not Achieved, Achieved (A), Merit (M), or Excellence (E). You need 80 credits to pass each level — and 50 Merit-or-Excellence credits to earn endorsement.

Credit totals by level

Level 1: 80 credits at Level 1+ (incl. 10 literacy + 10 numeracy)
Level 2: 60 credits at Level 2+ plus 20 from L1 = 80
Level 3: 60 credits at Level 3+ plus 20 from L2 = 80

Endorsement

Each level can be endorsed with Merit (50+ credits at M or E) or Excellence (50+ credits at E). Endorsements apply at the qualification level — so 50 Level 3 Merit credits gets you a Merit-endorsed Level 3 certificate.

University Entrance (UE)

UE is the minimum bar for enrolling at a NZ university. You need:

  • NCEA Level 3
  • 14 credits at L3 in three approved subjects (UE-approved subject list)
  • UE Literacy — 10 credits at L2 or above: 5 reading + 5 writing
  • UE Numeracy — 10 credits at L1 or above

From there, individual courses (Engineering, Medicine, Law) layer on a higher Rank Score requirement using your best 80 credits at L3.

Frequently Asked Questions

How many credits do I need to pass NCEA Level 1? ▾
80 credits at Level 1 or higher, including 10 literacy credits and 10 numeracy credits from approved standards. From 2024 these are met through the new co-requisite assessments.
How many credits for NCEA Level 2? ▾
80 credits in total — 60 must be at Level 2 or higher, and up to 20 can be carried up from Level 1. The Level 1 literacy and numeracy requirements (or the new co-requisite) must also be met.
How do I get NCEA endorsement? ▾
50 credits at Merit or Excellence at the level of the qualification gets you Merit endorsement. 50 credits at Excellence specifically gets you Excellence endorsement.
What is University Entrance? ▾
UE is the minimum requirement to enrol at a NZ university. You need NCEA Level 3, 14 credits in each of three approved Level 3 subjects, UE Literacy (10 credits at L2+ — 5 reading and 5 writing), and UE Numeracy (10 credits at L1+).
