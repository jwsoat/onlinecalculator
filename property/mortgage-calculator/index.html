NZ LVR Rules

The Reserve Bank of New Zealand (RBNZ) sets loan-to-value ratio (LVR) restrictions limiting how much banks can lend on low-deposit loans.

Current LVR Speed Limits

Owner-occupiers: Banks can lend no more than 20% of new lending to borrowers with deposits under 20% (LVR > 80%).

Investors: Maximum LVR is typically 65%–70% (deposit of 30%–35% required), though exemptions exist for new builds.

First Home Buyer Help

Kāinga Ora First Home Loan: Lets eligible buyers get on the ladder with just 5% deposit.
First Home Grant: Up to $10,000 toward your deposit (income and price caps apply).
KiwiSaver withdrawal: After 3 years of contributions, withdraw all but $1,000 toward your first home.

FAQ

What is a "high LVR" loan? ▾
An LVR above 80% (deposit under 20%) is considered high. Banks often charge a Low-Equity Premium (LEP) of 0.25%–1.5% extra interest on high-LVR owner-occupier loans.
Can I use KiwiSaver and First Home Grant together? ▾
Yes. You can combine a KiwiSaver first-home withdrawal with the First Home Grant and a Kāinga Ora First Home Loan if you meet each scheme's eligibility criteria.
What is the minimum deposit to buy a house in NZ? ▾
Most NZ banks require a 20% deposit for existing homes (80% LVR limit set by the RBNZ). First-home buyers may qualify for a 5% deposit via the Kāinga Ora First Home Loan if income and house price caps are met. Some banks allow 10% deposits on new builds. A larger deposit means lower repayments and avoids low-equity margins added to your interest rate.
