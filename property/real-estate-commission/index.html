How NZ real estate commission works

New Zealand agencies almost universally charge tiered commission. The rate on the first $400k–$500k of the sale is higher (typically 3.5%–4%), then drops on the remaining balance (typically 2%–2.75%). On top of that there's usually a flat base/administration fee and 15% GST.

Common formula

Tier 1: min(sale_price, threshold) × tier_1_rate
Tier 2: max(sale_price − threshold, 0) × tier_2_rate
Subtotal: tier_1 + tier_2 + base_fee
Total: subtotal × 1.15 + marketing

What about marketing?

Marketing (photography, signage, premium TradeMe Property and realestate.co.nz listings, auctioneer fees) is almost always billed separately and is often payable up-front whether or not the property sells. Get this in writing before signing the agency agreement.

Can you negotiate?

Yes — particularly on properties over $1m where percentage commissions add up fast, or in slower market conditions. Get at least three appraisals so you have leverage. Lower-fee or fixed-fee agencies (e.g. Tall Poppy, 200Square) advertise structures well below the traditional tiered model — plug their numbers into the calculator above to compare like-for-like.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the average real estate commission in NZ? ▾
NZ real estate commissions are typically tiered. A common structure is around 4% on the first $500,000 of the sale price, then 2.5% on the remainder, plus a flat base fee (often $500–$1,000) and 15% GST. Marketing and auction costs are charged separately.
Do I pay GST on real estate agent commission? ▾
Yes. As a private vendor you cannot claim it back. GST of 15% is added to the agent's total commission and base fee. Always confirm whether quoted rates are GST-inclusive or GST-exclusive before signing.
Is the commission rate negotiable? ▾
Yes — both the tier rates and the base fee can be negotiated, especially in a slower market or for properties worth over $1 million where the percentage applies to a large dollar amount.
Are marketing costs included in commission? ▾
Usually no. Photography, signage, premium listings on TradeMe/realestate.co.nz, and auctioneer fees are billed separately and are often payable upfront, regardless of whether the property sells.
