The Rent vs Buy Decision in NZ

NZ has historically rewarded buyers — property prices have roughly doubled every 10 years on average for decades. But the answer depends heavily on three things: how long you'll stay, capital growth, and what return you'd otherwise earn on the deposit.

Hidden Costs of Buying

Beyond the mortgage: legal fees ($1,500–$3,000), LIM and building inspection ($600–$1,200), council rates ($2,000–$4,500/yr), insurance ($1,500–$3,000/yr), maintenance (~1% of property value/yr), and selling costs of 3% if you eventually sell.

Hidden Costs of Renting

You miss out on capital growth, but also pay no rates, no insurance, no maintenance. The "opportunity cost" of a $160k deposit invested at 5% is ~$8,000/yr you'd earn instead.

FAQ

How long should I plan to stay to make buying worthwhile? ▾
As a rule of thumb in NZ: 5+ years for buying to break even on transaction costs alone. 7–10+ years for capital growth to clearly out-pace rent + opportunity cost. Shorter horizons usually favour renting.
Does this account for tax? ▾
For your own home (owner-occupied), no capital gains tax applies in NZ. The opportunity cost of investing the deposit elsewhere would be taxed (PIE/RWT). This calculator simplifies tax treatment.
