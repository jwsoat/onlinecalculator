The NZ Bright-Line Test

The bright-line test taxes profits on residential property sold within a set period of buying it, even if the seller wasn't running a property business. It's not capital gains tax — it's added to your normal income and taxed at your marginal rate.

The 2-Year Rule (from 1 July 2024)

For residential property acquired on or after 1 July 2024, the bright-line period is 2 years. Sold within 2 years of purchase? Any gain is taxable. Sold after 2 years? No bright-line tax.

Previous Rules

Property bought between 27 March 2021 and 30 June 2024 was caught by a 10-year (5-year for new builds) bright-line test, since wound back to 2 years. The rule that applies depends on the date you acquired the property.

Main Home Exclusion

Your main home is usually exempt — but only if you've lived in it for most of the bright-line period. Investors and bach owners aren't covered by this exclusion.

FAQ

When does the "acquisition date" start? ▾
Usually the date the title transferred to you (settlement). For off-the-plan purchases the date of the binding contract may apply. Get tax advice for complex situations.
What if I lose money on the sale? ▾
Losses within the bright-line period can be ring-fenced — they only offset gains on other bright-line property sales, not your wages.
Does this calculator replace tax advice? ▾
No. This is an indicative calculator. Bright-line rules have exemptions, special cases (relationship property, inheritance, subdivision) and rolling rules that require an accountant or IRD-published guidance.
