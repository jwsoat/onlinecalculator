Gross vs Net Yield

Gross yield = annual rent ÷ purchase price × 100. It's the "advertising" yield agents quote, but it ignores all costs.

Net yield subtracts real-world holding costs: council rates, insurance, property management fees, maintenance, vacancies, and (sometimes) accountant fees. It's the true cash return before mortgage interest and tax.

What's a Good Yield in NZ?

Auckland and Wellington investors often accept 3–4% gross yield, betting on capital growth. Provincial markets like Invercargill, Whanganui, or Rotorua can offer 5–7% gross. Net yields are usually 1–2 percentage points lower.

NZ Tax: Interest Deductibility

From April 2024 onward, interest on residential investment loans is being progressively restored as a deductible expense, reaching 100% deductibility in the 2025–26 tax year. Consult an accountant — rules change.

FAQ

Does net yield include the mortgage? ▾
No. Net yield is a property-level metric — it shows what the property returns regardless of how it's financed. Cash-on-cash return is a separate metric that includes mortgage interest.
What expenses should I include? ▾
Rates, insurance, property management, maintenance/repairs (budget 1% of property value annually), vacancy allowance (~2 weeks/year), body corp (if apartment), water if landlord-paid, and accountancy fees.
