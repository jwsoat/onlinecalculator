# Outreach Email Templates — Tier A Targets

_For onlinecalculator.co.nz backlink campaign. Use from info@jwsoat.com or set up a hello@onlinecalculator.co.nz before sending — the latter converts ~30% better._

## Principles before you send anything

1. **Personalise the first line.** It must reference something specific you read on their site. Generic openers ("I love your blog!") get binned.
2. **Keep under 150 words.** They read these on a phone between meetings.
3. **One clear ask.** Not three.
4. **Give them an easy "no".** No follow-up guilt-trip.
5. **Never write "I hope this finds you well", "I'm reaching out to", "synergies", or "quick question"** — all spam-classifier flags + reader fatigue.
6. **Send from a real person, real signature, real phone number.** Cold emails without phone numbers convert worse.
7. **Cadence:** 1 follow-up after 7 days. Then stop. Two follow-ups is fine, three is harassment.

## Template 8 — Reddit (r/PersonalFinanceNZ)

Don't email Reddit — earn citations by being useful. Spend 30 min/week answering questions where a calculator genuinely helps. **Don't link on your first 10 comments.** Build account history. Then:

```
Worked it out for you:

[Specific numbers with breakdown]

Used [your specific tool] — onlinecalculator.co.nz/finance/paye-calculator
— but [competitor like paye.net.nz] gives the same answer if you'd
rather. Either way you're paying about $X in secondary tax.
```

**Why this works:** Naming a competitor signals you're not just promoting; gives the answer first, link second. Mods don't remove this.

## Before you send anything — checklist

- [ ] Email signature has real name, site URL, phone number
- [ ] Sending from a domain email (info@onlinecalculator.co.nz or similar), not gmail
- [ ] SPF/DKIM/DMARC set up on your domain (avoids spam folder — check at https://mxtoolbox.com)
- [ ] Sender warmed up — if it's a fresh inbox, send a few normal emails first before cold outreach
- [ ] Each email personalised on line 1 — never copy/paste without changing it
- [ ] Spelled their name right (the #1 reason emails get ignored)
