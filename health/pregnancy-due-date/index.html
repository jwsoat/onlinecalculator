How Due Date Is Calculated

The most common method is Naegele's rule: add 280 days (40 weeks) to the first day of your last menstrual period (LMP). This assumes a 28-day cycle and conception roughly 2 weeks after LMP. We adjust automatically if your cycle is longer or shorter.

Trimesters

1st trimester: weeks 1–13. 2nd trimester: weeks 14–27. 3rd trimester: weeks 28–40+.

NZ Maternity Care

In NZ you can choose a Lead Maternity Carer (LMC) — usually a midwife — for free pregnancy and birth care. Register with one as early as possible. You may be entitled to paid parental leave (currently 26 weeks).

FAQ

How accurate is the due date? ▾
Only ~5% of babies are born on their exact due date. About 80% arrive within 2 weeks of it. An early-pregnancy ultrasound dating scan (usually 8–13 weeks in NZ) gives the most accurate estimate.
What if my periods are irregular? ▾
If your cycle isn't a regular 28 days, this calculator adjusts using your cycle length. For irregular cycles, an early dating ultrasound is the gold standard. Talk to your midwife or GP.
