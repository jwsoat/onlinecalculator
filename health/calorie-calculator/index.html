BMR & TDEE Explained

BMR (Basal Metabolic Rate) = calories burned at rest, just to keep your body running.
TDEE (Total Daily Energy Expenditure) = BMR × activity multiplier. This is your "maintenance" intake — eat this much and your weight stays steady.

Mifflin-St Jeor Formula

Men: BMR = 10 × kg + 6.25 × cm − 5 × age + 5
Women: BMR = 10 × kg + 6.25 × cm − 5 × age − 161

NZ Kilojoules

NZ food labels use kilojoules (kJ), not calories. 1 kcal = 4.184 kJ. The MoH suggests an average adult eats around 8,700 kJ/day (~2,080 kcal). Actual needs vary widely.

NZ Healthy Eating Guidelines

The Ministry of Health recommends: lots of vegetables and fruit, choose whole grains, eat protein with each meal (legumes, fish, eggs, lean meat), limit highly processed and high-sugar foods. Calories are one variable — food quality matters too.

FAQ

How fast should I lose weight? ▾
A 0.5–1 kg per week loss (a deficit of ~500–1000 kcal/day from TDEE) is usually sustainable. Faster losses often come back fast or cause muscle loss.
Is this accurate? ▾
Mifflin-St Jeor is the most accurate population formula but individual variation is ±10–15%. Adjust your intake based on real-world results over 2–4 weeks.
