What "Ideal Weight" Really Means

There's no single "perfect" weight. The four classic formulas (Devine, Robinson, Miller, Hamwi) were originally created for pharmaceutical dosing, not aesthetic targets. They tend to give a single number for each height + sex.

For most people the BMI healthy range (18.5–24.9) is more useful — it gives a range you can fall anywhere within, accounting for muscle and frame size. Athletes with high muscle mass may sit above the range while still being lean and healthy.

Better Markers Than Weight Alone

Waist circumference, waist-to-height ratio (should be < 0.5), body composition (DXA scans available at some NZ clinics), and fitness markers like resting heart rate often track health better than weight on its own.

FAQ

Why do the four formulas give different answers? ▾
They were derived from different populations decades apart. Devine is the original; the others tried to improve fit for shorter and taller adults. None were designed for modern body-composition assessment.
