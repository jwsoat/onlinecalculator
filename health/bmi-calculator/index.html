Adjusted Thresholds for Māori & Pacific Peoples

Some NZ health guidelines use higher thresholds for Māori and Pacific peoples: overweight ≥ 26, obese ≥ 32. This reflects body composition differences. Your GP can interpret BMI in the context of muscle mass, waist circumference, and overall health.

BMI Limitations

BMI is a screening tool, not a diagnostic. It doesn't distinguish between muscle and fat, so athletes often score "overweight" or "obese" despite being lean. Waist-to-height ratio and waist circumference are often better indicators of metabolic risk.

FAQ

What is the BMI formula? ▾
BMI = weight (kg) ÷ height (m)². For imperial: BMI = weight (lb) ÷ height (in)² × 703.
Is BMI accurate for children? ▾
No — children need age-and-sex-specific BMI percentile charts, not adult cut-offs. See your GP or Plunket nurse for child weight assessments.
