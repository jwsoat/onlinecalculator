How NZ Road User Charges work

Petrol-powered cars pay road tax via fuel excise (~70c per litre). Vehicles that don't use petrol — diesel cars, EVs, and plug-in hybrids — pay via Road User Charges (RUC) instead. RUC is bought in 1,000 km blocks from NZTA / Waka Kotahi, and your distance is verified at WoF inspections via the odometer.

2026 light-vehicle rates

Light EV (≤3,500 kg): $76 per 1,000 km (7.6¢/km)
Plug-in hybrid: $53 per 1,000 km (PHEVs also pay petrol excise)
Light diesel: $76 per 1,000 km

Heavy vehicles

Heavy vehicles pay much higher rates based on their Gross Vehicle Mass (GVM) band and axle configuration. Rates range from ~$100/1,000 km for small trucks up to $600+ for the largest combinations. Always check the current NZTA RUC rates schedule before buying for commercial use.

When was EV RUC introduced?

EVs were exempt from RUC until 1 April 2024, when the government ended the exemption. PHEVs joined the regime on the same date at a reduced rate, because they also pay petrol excise on the petrol portion of their fuel use.

Frequently Asked Questions

How much is RUC for an EV in NZ? ▾
Light EVs (≤3,500 kg) pay $76 per 1,000 km — the same rate as light diesel vehicles. PHEVs pay $53 per 1,000 km because they also pay fuel excise on petrol.
Do petrol cars pay RUC? ▾
No. Petrol vehicles pay road tax through fuel excise built into the petrol price. RUC applies only to diesel, EV, and plug-in hybrid vehicles.
How do I buy RUC? ▾
Buy RUC online from NZTA / Waka Kotahi, at NZ Post outlets, AA centres, or through approved RUC vendors. You buy in 1,000 km blocks and your distance is verified at WoF / CoF inspections via your odometer reading.
Is RUC cheaper than petrol? ▾
For most light vehicles, an EV at 7.6¢/km RUC costs less per kilometre than a typical petrol car at ~20¢/km — even before counting the lower electricity cost vs petrol. Use the calculator above to compare your specific numbers.

💡 RUC Tip

Buy RUC in larger blocks (10,000+ km) to reduce the per-block admin fee, and always update your odometer reading at WoF time to avoid back-charges.
