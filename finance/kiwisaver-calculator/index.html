How KiwiSaver Works

KiwiSaver is New Zealand's workplace retirement savings scheme. As an employee you contribute a chosen percentage of your gross salary, your employer matches at least 3.5% (from 1 April 2026), and the government tops up your account by up to $260.72 per year.

The Three Sources of Contributions

1. You: Choose 3.5%, 4%, 6%, 8% or 10% of your gross salary, deducted via PAYE. The minimum rose from 3% to 3.5% on 1 April 2026.

2. Your employer: Must contribute at least 3.5% of your gross salary (less ESCT tax) from 1 April 2026.

3. The government: Pays 50¢ for every $1 you contribute, up to $260.72 per year. To maximise this, contribute at least $521.44 yourself between 1 July and 30 June each year.

Default Funds and Returns

Choosing the right fund type matters enormously over 30+ years. Conservative funds typically return 3–4% p.a. after fees; balanced funds 4–6%; growth funds 6–8%. A 1% higher annual return over 30 years roughly doubles your final balance.

FAQ

When can I withdraw KiwiSaver? ▾
From age 65, or earlier for first-home purchase (after 3 years of membership), significant financial hardship, serious illness, or permanent emigration.
Can I use KiwiSaver for my first home? ▾
Yes. After 3 years of contributions, you can withdraw all but $1,000 of your KiwiSaver balance (excluding government contributions) toward a first-home deposit. You may also qualify for a First Home Grant of up to $10,000.
What if I'm self-employed? ▾
Self-employed and non-earners can still join KiwiSaver and contribute voluntarily. Contributing at least $521.44 per year still earns you the full $260.72 government contribution.
