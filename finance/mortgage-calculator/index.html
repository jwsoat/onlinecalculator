How NZ Mortgage Repayments Are Calculated

NZ home loans typically use the standard principal-and-interest amortisation formula. Each repayment covers part of the interest charged plus a slice of the principal. Early on, most of the payment is interest; as the balance falls, more goes to principal.

The Formula

Monthly repayment: M = P × r × (1 + r)n ÷ ((1 + r)n − 1)
where P = loan amount, r = monthly rate (annual ÷ 12), n = total months

Tip: Pay Fortnightly

Switching from monthly to fortnightly repayments and paying half the monthly amount each fortnight means you make 26 fortnightly payments — equivalent to 13 monthly payments per year, instead of 12. On a 30-year loan this typically shaves 4–6 years off the term and tens of thousands off the interest bill.

Current NZ Mortgage Rates

NZ home loan rates fluctuate with the Reserve Bank's Official Cash Rate. As of 2026, advertised 1-year fixed rates from major banks range roughly between 5.5% and 6.8%. Always shop around — even a 0.2% difference matters over 30 years.

Frequently Asked Questions

How much deposit do I need in NZ? ▾
Most NZ banks require a 20% deposit on owner-occupied loans (LVR limit), though first-home buyers can sometimes get 5%–10% deposit loans via Kāinga Ora First Home Loan or Welcome Home Loan schemes. Investors typically need a 35%–40% deposit.
Should I fix or float? ▾
Fixed rates give certainty for 1–5 years; floating rates change with the OCR. Many NZ borrowers split — fixing a portion for stability and floating a portion for flexibility (extra repayments without break fees).
How does my term affect total interest? ▾
A 25-year term has higher monthly payments but much less interest than 30 years. On a $600k loan at 6.5%, a 30-year term costs ~$764k in interest versus ~$615k over 25 years — a $149k saving.

💡 LVR Quick Guide

LVR = Loan ÷ Property Value. NZ banks generally cap owner-occupied LVR at 80% (20% deposit). Properties bought with <20% deposit may incur Low-Equity Premium (LEP) of 0.25%–1.5% extra interest.
