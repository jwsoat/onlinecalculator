About NZ Personal Loans

Personal loan interest rates in New Zealand typically range from 7% (secured car loans from main banks) to 25%+ (unsecured loans from finance companies). Always compare the annual percentage rate (APR), which includes fees, not just the headline rate.

Secured vs Unsecured

Secured loans use an asset (usually a vehicle) as collateral, so rates are lower — typically 7%–12% in NZ. Unsecured personal loans have no collateral, so rates run 12%–22%.

Watch the Total Cost

A 5-year loan vs 3-year loan at the same rate can almost double the total interest paid. Shorter terms mean higher repayments but far less interest.

FAQ

Can I pay off a loan early? ▾
Most NZ personal loans allow extra repayments without penalty (check the contract). Paying $50–$100 extra each fortnight on a 5-year loan can shave months off the term and hundreds in interest.
Does my credit score matter? ▾
Yes — significantly. NZ lenders use Centrix or Equifax reports. A "good" or "excellent" score (700+) often qualifies for the lowest advertised rate; lower scores attract risk-based pricing.
