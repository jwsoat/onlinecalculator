The Power of Compounding

Compound interest is interest earned on both your original investment and on the interest already accumulated. Albert Einstein reportedly called it "the eighth wonder of the world." For long-term savers it transforms small regular contributions into large balances.

The Formula

A = P × (1 + r/n)nt
P = principal, r = annual rate, n = compounds per year, t = years

NZ Term Deposit Rates

Bank term deposit rates in NZ vary from ~3% (call accounts) to 5%–6% (1–5 year fixed). Returns are taxed as Resident Withholding Tax (RWT) at your marginal rate.

FAQ

Is investment return taxed in NZ? ▾
Yes. Interest is taxed as RWT at your marginal rate. KiwiSaver and PIE funds are taxed at your Prescribed Investor Rate (PIR), capped at 28%. Capital gains on shares held long-term are generally not taxed for individuals.
What's a realistic return assumption? ▾
Conservative funds: 3–4% p.a. after fees. Balanced: 4–6%. Growth/share funds: 6–8% long-term average. Past performance isn't a guarantee — markets are volatile year-to-year.
