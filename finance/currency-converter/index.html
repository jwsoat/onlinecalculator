About the NZ Dollar

The New Zealand dollar (NZD) — nicknamed the "Kiwi" — is the world's 10th-most-traded currency. It moves with dairy prices (Fonterra is a major exporter), the Reserve Bank's Official Cash Rate, and global risk sentiment (the NZD is a "risk-on" currency).

Tip: Avoid Airport Currency Booths

Airport bureaux often charge 5–10% spreads. Travel debit cards (Wise, Revolver, Air NZ OneSmart), most NZ bank debit/credit cards used abroad, and ATM withdrawals at the destination usually offer rates within 1% of mid-market.

FAQ

Are these rates live? ▾
No. The rates here are indicative reference values updated periodically. For booked-rate live conversion always check xe.com, Wise, or your bank app at the moment of transaction.
What's the difference between mid-market and retail rate? ▾
The mid-market rate is the wholesale interbank rate. Banks and brokers add a spread (their margin), so the rate you receive when buying or selling is always slightly worse than mid-market. Spreads vary from 0.3% (online money transfer services) to 4%+ (airport bureaux).
