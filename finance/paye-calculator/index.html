2025–2026 NZ Tax Brackets

New Zealand uses a progressive tax system. You only pay the higher rate on income above each threshold.

Income BandTax Rate
$0 – $15,60010.5%
$15,601 – $53,50017.5%
$53,501 – $78,10030%
$78,101 – $180,00033%
Over $180,00039%

Student Loan Repayments

If you have an NZ student loan, you repay 12% of income above the threshold. The 2025–26 annual threshold is $24,128 ($464/week, $928/fortnight). Repayments are deducted automatically by your employer when you use a tax code ending in "SL".

Independent Earner Tax Credit (IETC)

The IETC gives up to $520/year ($10/week) to NZ tax residents earning $24,000–$70,000 who don't receive Working for Families or a student-loan-related benefit. The full $520 applies from $24,000 to $66,000; above $66,000 it abates 13c per $1 of extra income and is fully gone by $70,000. Pick the ME tax code to claim it. (Thresholds lifted from $44k/$48k in Budget 2024.)

ACC Earner Levy 2025–26

The ACC earner levy is $1.67 per $100 of liable earnings (1.67%), capped at annual earnings of $142,283. Maximum levy payable is $2,376.12 per year.

Payroll Giving

If your employer offers payroll giving, donations to registered charities deducted via PAYE earn an immediate 33.33% tax credit. A $1,000 annual donation effectively costs you only ~$667 because $333 of your tax is rebated.

Frequently Asked Questions

What's "Total Remuneration" and how do I know which to choose? ▾
Most NZ offer letters quote a gross-only salary — e.g. "$80,000 plus 3.5% employer KiwiSaver." Keep the toggle on "Gross only." Some employers (senior roles, government, startups) instead offer "Total Remuneration / TR" — a single all-in figure that already includes the employer KiwiSaver. $80,000 TR means your actual gross is $80,000 ÷ 1.035 ≈ $77,295. Check your offer letter — it should say which model applies.
When do I use a secondary tax code? ▾
When you have more than one job. Your main employer uses M (or ME). Each additional employer uses S/SH/ST/SA based on your total income across all jobs. The secondary code is a flat rate so you don't double-count the lower brackets.
Does KiwiSaver reduce my taxable income? ▾
No. NZ KiwiSaver contributions are deducted from your after-tax pay, so they don't reduce your PAYE.
What's 8% holiday pay? ▾
Casual and fixed-term workers can be paid an extra 8% on top of their gross wages each pay, in lieu of accruing 4 weeks of annual leave (8% ≈ 4 ÷ 52). Permanent staff usually accrue leave rather than receive 8% each pay.
What's the IETC? ▾
The Independent Earner Tax Credit — up to $520/year ($10/week) — for NZ tax residents earning $24,000–$70,000 who don't receive Working for Families or student-loan-related benefits. Full credit applies up to $66,000; abates 13c per $1 between $66,000 and $70,000.
Is the top tax rate in NZ really 39%? ▾
Yes. The 39% rate applies to income above $180,000 per year, introduced 1 April 2021. A $180,000 salary has an effective rate of about 30.2% because most income is taxed at lower bands.
