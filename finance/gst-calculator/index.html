How NZ GST Works

New Zealand's Goods and Services Tax (GST) is a broad-based consumption tax of 15%, introduced in 1986. It applies to almost all goods and services sold in New Zealand. Businesses registered for GST must collect it on sales and can claim it back on business expenses.

GST Formulas

Adding GST: GST-inclusive price = Original × 1.15
Removing GST: GST amount = Inclusive ÷ 1.15 × 0.15
GST portion: Inclusive ÷ 1.15 = Excl. GST price

Do You Need to Register for GST?

You must register for GST with Inland Revenue (IRD) if your taxable turnover exceeds $60,000 in any 12-month period. Voluntary registration is available for businesses below this threshold.

GST on Common Transactions

Most goods and services attract GST at 15%. Key exceptions include:

✅ Zero-rated (0%): Exports, financial services (some), donated goods sold by charities

❌ Exempt: Residential rents, donations, certain financial services

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the current NZ GST rate? ▾
New Zealand's GST rate is 15% and has been since 1 October 2010, when it was raised from 12.5%. There are no current plans to change this rate.
How do I calculate GST on an inclusive price? ▾
Divide the GST-inclusive price by 1.15 to get the GST-exclusive price. Multiply that by 0.15 to get the GST component. For example: $115 ÷ 1.15 = $100 (excl. GST), and $100 × 0.15 = $15 (GST).
When do I have to register for GST? ▾
You must register for GST with IRD when your turnover from taxable activities reaches $60,000 in any 12-month period. You can also register voluntarily below this threshold.
How often do I file GST returns? ▾
GST returns can be filed monthly, two-monthly, or six-monthly. Most businesses file every two months. If your turnover is over $24 million per year, you must file monthly.
