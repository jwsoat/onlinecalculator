Markup vs margin — they're not the same

Markup and margin describe the same dollar of profit but from different starting points. Markup is profit as a percentage of cost. Margin is profit as a percentage of selling price. Because the selling price is always larger than the cost, the margin percentage is always smaller than the markup percentage.

Formulas

Markup % = (Price − Cost) ÷ Cost × 100
Margin % = (Price − Cost) ÷ Price × 100
Convert: Margin = Markup ÷ (1 + Markup)

Quick reference

Markup 25%  → Margin 20.0%
Markup 50%  → Margin 33.3%
Markup 100% → Margin 50.0%
Markup 200% → Margin 66.7%

NZ context

Always work in GST-exclusive numbers when calculating markup/margin. GST is added on top at 15% for the customer-facing total. Tradies typically apply a markup of 25%–50% on materials and bill labour at a separate charge-out rate.

Frequently Asked Questions

What's the difference between markup and margin? ▾
Markup is profit as a percentage of cost; margin is profit as a percentage of selling price. A 50% markup is the same as a 33.3% margin — same dollars, different base.
How do I convert markup to margin? ▾
Margin = Markup ÷ (1 + Markup). So a 50% markup = 50% ÷ 1.50 = 33.3% margin.
What's a good markup for tradies? ▾
Most NZ tradies use 25–50% markup on materials and parts. Labour is billed separately at an hourly charge-out rate that covers overhead, vehicle costs, and target profit.
Does GST affect markup? ▾
Always work in GST-exclusive numbers. Then add 15% GST on top of your final GST-exclusive selling price for the customer total.
