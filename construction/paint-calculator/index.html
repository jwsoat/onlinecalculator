NZ Paint Coverage Rates

Most quality NZ interior paints (Resene, Dulux, Wattyl) cover 10–14 m² per litre on a smooth surface in one coat. Use the lower end for porous primer coats, textured surfaces, or rough timber. Use the higher end for second coats on already-primed walls.

How Many Coats?

Most jobs need 2 coats over a primer/undercoat. Painting a light colour over a dark colour often needs 3 coats or a tinted undercoat. Patch-touch-ups are usually fine with 1 coat if you have the original tin.

Don't Forget Primer

New gib, raw timber, MDF, and patches all need primer/sealer. Resene Quick Dry, Dulux 1Step or Wattyl Killrust are common NZ choices. Primer is in addition to topcoat litres.

FAQ

Should I roller or brush? ▾
Roller for flat wall areas — much faster and a smoother finish. Brush (or a small "cut-in" brush) for edges, corners, trim, and around fittings. Spray for large external jobs or factory-style finishes.
What sheen for what room? ▾
Matte/flat: ceilings, low-traffic walls. Low-sheen/eggshell: living rooms, bedrooms. Semi-gloss: kitchens, bathrooms, trim. Gloss: exterior trim, doors, durable surfaces.
