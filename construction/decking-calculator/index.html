Decking Timber Options in NZ

Pine (H3.2 treated): Cheapest, paint or stain. Lifespan 15–25 years with regular maintenance.

Kwila / Merbau: Hardwood imported from SE Asia. Lasts 30+ years. Watch for tannin runoff on first wash.

Vitex: Pacific hardwood, lighter than kwila. Sustainable plantation supply.

Composite (Eva-Last, Modwood, Trex): Recycled plastic + wood fibre. No oiling needed. Higher upfront cost.

NZ Building Code

Decks under 1.5 m above ground generally don't need consent in NZ, but check with your council. Above that, you'll need building consent and the structure must comply with NZS 3604 and barrier height rules (1 m minimum).

FAQ

What joist spacing should I use? ▾
450 mm centres is the NZ standard for residential decks with 19–22 mm decking. Reduce to 400 mm for spas, heavy planters, or hot tub support. 600 mm is the max for 32 mm thick boards.
Screws or hidden clips? ▾
Stainless steel screws (304 grade min, 316 for coastal) are reliable and easy. Hidden-fastener clips (Eva-Last, Camo) give a clean look with no visible holes — costs more, slower to install, but a much higher-end finish.
