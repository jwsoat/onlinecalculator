Building a timber retaining wall in NZ

This calculator assumes a standard NZ DIY retaining wall: vertical H5-treated posts set in concrete footings, with horizontal H4 timber rails (often 200×50 or 150×50) fixed to the uphill face. For walls up to 1.5 m exposed height with no load above, no building consent is generally required (Schedule 1 of the Building Act 2004). Walls over 1.5 m, or any wall supporting a driveway / structure / fence, need consent and an engineer's design.

Key rules of thumb

Post length: exposed height + (height for walls <1m: ⅓; walls >1m: ½) embedded in ground
Post spacing: typically 1.2–1.8 m (closer for taller walls)
Footing volume: π × r² × depth per post
Bag yield: ~0.01 m³ per 20 kg bag of premix concrete

Drainage — non-negotiable

Always backfill the first 300 mm behind the wall with clean drainage gravel (GAP20, scoria, or pea metal) and run a slotted drainage pipe (Novaflo) along the base out to daylight or a soak pit. Without drainage, hydrostatic pressure from saturated soil after winter rain will eventually push the wall over.

Timber treatment

Posts in ground contact must be H5. Rails above ground can be H4. Anything less (H3, untreated) will rot within years in NZ's wet conditions. Buy from a yard, check the treatment stamp on each piece.

Frequently Asked Questions

Do I need consent for a retaining wall in NZ? ▾
Walls under 1.5 m high that don't support a load (vehicle, building, fence) are generally exempt under Schedule 1 of the Building Act 2004. Walls over 1.5 m, or any wall with a load above, require building consent and a chartered engineer's design.
What timber should I use? ▾
H5-treated for posts and any timber in ground contact. H4 is acceptable for rails above ground. Anything less will rot in NZ conditions.
How deep should footings be? ▾
One-third of the post height for walls under 1 m, half the height for walls 1 m and over. For a 1.2 m exposed wall, use ~1.8 m posts with 600 mm in concrete.
Do I need drainage behind it? ▾
Yes — always. Backfill 300 mm with clean drainage gravel and install a slotted Novaflo pipe at the base to daylight. Skipping this is the #1 cause of failed retaining walls.
