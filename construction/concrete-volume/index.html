Choosing the Right Concrete Strength

NZ concrete is graded by MPa (megapascals at 28 days):

20 MPa – paths, light slabs, garden walls
25 MPa – house slabs, driveways
30 MPa – heavily loaded slabs, suspended slabs, structural
40 MPa+ – industrial/commercial, marine exposure

Bag vs Truck

Bagged concrete is fine for jobs under ~1 m³ (around 110 × 20 kg bags). For larger pours, order a truck — typically much cheaper per m³ and the finish is more consistent. Allow access for an 8m truck.

FAQ

How long does concrete take to cure? ▾
Walk-on hard after 24 hours. Light vehicle in 7 days. Full strength at 28 days. Keep slabs damp for the first 3–7 days, especially in NZ summer.
Do I need reinforcing mesh? ▾
Most NZ house slabs need 668 mesh or D12 bars per structural drawings. Always follow your engineer's spec — under NZ Building Code, residential slabs typically need engineer-approved reinforcement.
