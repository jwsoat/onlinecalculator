## 4. Broken link building — specific process

Use this Google query family on competitors:
```
site:moneyhub.co.nz "calculator"
site:sorted.org.nz "calculator"
site:stuff.co.nz "calculator" inurl:money
```
Then run each result through https://www.deadlinkchecker.com — any 404 outbound link to a calculator is a swap opportunity. Email the page owner: "Hi, noticed your link to X is broken, here's a working alternative at onlinecalculator.co.nz/…"

Realistic conversion: ~5–10% reply, ~2–3% link. Send 50/week → ~1–2 links/week.

## 5. Linkable assets you should build (to attract links, not chase them)

Calculator sites earn links best through **data + tools journalists cite**. Build these:

1. **"NZ Take-Home Pay by Region" study** — use your PAYE calc + StatsNZ income data. Pitch to Stuff Money.
2. **"How Much KiwiSaver You'll Have at 65 vs What You Need" interactive** — every Kiwi cares; high virality.
3. **"True Cost of an Auckland Mortgage 2026"** — mortgage calc + Council rates data. Real estate writers love this.
4. **GST Calculator with Industry Templates** — landing pages for tradies/freelancers/Airbnb hosts → community link bait.
5. **A free embeddable widget** — let bloggers embed your calculator with attribution link. This is how calculate.co.nz scaled.

## 6. Anchor text strategy (when you control it)

Aim for natural diversity:
- 30% branded: "onlinecalculator.co.nz", "Online Calculator NZ"
- 30% URL/naked: "https://onlinecalculator.co.nz/finance/paye-calculator/"
- 25% generic: "this calculator", "this tool", "click here"
- 15% partial-match: "NZ PAYE calculator", "free GST calculator NZ"

Avoid: >5% exact-match commercial anchors ("best paye calculator nz"). That's the spam signal Penguin watches for.

## 7. What NOT to do

- ❌ Fiverr backlink packages
- ❌ Mass guest posting on low-quality sites
- ❌ Reciprocal link schemes with other calculator sites
- ❌ PBNs
- ❌ Comment spam
- ❌ Buying links

All of these will likely trigger Google's SpamBrain in 2026; some can attract a manual action.
